A Message from la presidenta:

Greetings, tangueros!

June is going to be a confusing month - we are moving the group lessons to a new day, a new time and a new location beginning immediately. The Group Class will meet on Thursdays, 7:00 - 9:00 (2 hours!), at Traditions Dance Studio. Traditions is located at the corner of Kingston Pike and Mohician St.- diagonally across Kingston Pike from Homberg Place (behind Antiques, Inc.). For the month of June, we will also continue to have Group classes at Dance Dimension on Wednesday at 7:30. SO - you may attend either or both during the month of June!

With a new start at Traditions, we will be going back to the beginning and working on the basics of Tango for an hour or so each week and then spending some time on new material as well as putting it all together to music!

The fee will remain at $5 per person. Unfortunately, I will not be able to continue to offer the first lesson free.

We will continue to have the 2nd and 4th Sunday Practicas at Dance Dimensions as in the past. In addition to the Group Class at Traditions, I will begin a structured 6-week class at Dance Dimensions on either Tuesdays or Wednesdays beginning the first week in August, and will most likely be from 7:30 - 9:00. This class will require per-registration. If there are not enough people interested in the class, it won't happen. I have to know before August 1 if there will be a class.

The 6-week class will be 1 1/2 hrs per week and the fee will be $45 per person for the entire 6-weeks (9 hours). That's $5 per hour!

The benefit of the structured class is that we will be able to make steady progress through the art of Tango. I have outlined a 6-week plan with some flexibility built in. My ultimate goal is for you to be able to dance Tango! We will probably plan a Milonga (party) for after the end of the class (everyone invited).

If there is sufficient interest, I will schedule a Level II 6-week class to take us beyond the first class - following a break after the first one.

I look forward to seeing all of you at the classes and/or practicias!

~Sheila

Argentine tango is a dance for the romantic at heart, for it contains the elements of embrace, interpretive movement and heart-stopping anticipation.

     Danced from the heart, There is a special nonverbal exchange of movement between partners that is improvised as the music is interpreted.

     Each partner focuses on even the slightest touch, movement or glance from the other.

     This is improvisational tango--social tango dancing at its best.         Bill Stillway
